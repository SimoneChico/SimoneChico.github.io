Toyota GR Yaris Rally1


The Toyota GR YARIS Rally1 HYBRID is a brand-new rally car designed to the revolutionary new-for-2022 FIA Rally1 technical regulations, which represent rallying's most significant technical overhaul for a generation. Rally1 cars replace World Rally Cars at the highest level of the WRC and feature a number of significant changes compared to their predecessors, including the introduction of technologies promoting sustainable motorsport. For the first time, the cars in rallying's top category have hybrid electric power. The existing 1.6-liter direct-injection turbocharged engine one of the only features of the previous generation of World Rally Cars to be carried over to Rally1 is paired with a hybrid unit that is common to every competing car.

Ford Puma Rally1


The Puma Hybrid Rally1 utilises an all new bespoke tubular T45 steel space frame chassis, providing an enhanced safety cell for the crew, a product of years of research and development undertaken in partnership with the FIA to further improve driver and co-driver safety standards. Marking a landmark moment in rally history, the Puma Hybrid Rally1 is the first hybrid FIA homologated car eligible for competition in the top category of the WRC. The battery-electric motor element of the hybrid powertrain captures energy during braking, storing it in a 3.9 kWh battery. The recovered energy can then be deployed via a 100kW electric motor in unison with the 1.6-litre EcoBoost engine to produce a total peak power output of over 500 bhp.

Hyundai I20 Rally1


The Hyundai i20 Rally1, built for the World Rally Championship (WRC) under Rally1 regulations, boasts a 1.6-liter turbocharged inline-4 engine delivering 500 hp alongside a 100 kW electric motor, forming a hybrid system with regenerative braking. Its 6-speed sequential gearbox and all-wheel-drive system with an active center differential ensure formidable performance on varied terrains. With a steel spaceframe chassis, lightweight composite bodywork, and advanced suspension offering up to 275 mm of wheel travel, it strikes a balance between agility and durability. Safety features include an integrated roll cage, FIA-standard seats, and harnesses. Packed with cutting-edge technology and designed for ultimate performance, the Hyundai i20 Rally1 is primed for the intense challenges of WRC competition.

TÄNAK SNATCHES RALLY ITALIA SARDEGNA WIN FROM
OGIER IN JOINT-CLOSEST WRC FINISH

Ott Tänak secured a surprising win at Rally Italia Sardegna, benefiting from Sébastien Ogier's misfortune as a damaged tire cost Ogier the lead in the final stage. Tänak, driving a Hyundai i20 N, clinched his first victory of the season after a close battle with Ogier, who had led since Saturday afternoon in his Toyota GR Yaris. Despite starting the rally-closing Wolf Power Stage with a 6.2-second lead, Ogier ended up 0.2 seconds behind Tänak due to the late setback.
